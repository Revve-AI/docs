# What Is a Voice Agent?

A **Voice Agent** (also called a callbot) is an AI-powered assistant that speaks with your customers over the phone or through an in-browser web call. It listens to what the caller says, understands their intent, and replies in natural speech — with the same business knowledge, tone, and guardrails you would give a trained human agent.

Voice Agents are designed for production telephony workloads: inbound hotlines, outbound campaigns, verification calls, and collections. They run continuously, scale to any call volume, and log every conversation for review.

## How a Call Works

When a call starts — whether the customer dials in or the system dials out — the Voice Agent:

1. **Greets the caller** with a configurable opening line.
2. **Transcribes speech to text** using the Speech Recognition (STT) model.
3. **Generates a reply** with the LLM, grounded in your instructions and knowledge base.
4. **Speaks the reply** through the Voice Synthesis (TTS) model.
5. **Handles interruptions, silence, and retries** based on your call-behavior rules.
6. **Transfers, ends, or continues** the call depending on the conversation outcome.

Every turn is saved as a transcript and enriched with a call summary, customer sentiment, and structured extracted fields for downstream systems.

## Supported Channels

A single Voice Agent can serve multiple channels at once:

| Channel | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Phone — Inbound** | Customers call a number connected to the agent (hotline, branch IVR replacement). |
| **Phone — Outbound** | The agent dials contacts from a campaign or API trigger. |
| **Web Call** | A browser-based audio widget for testing and embedded voice experiences. |
| **SIP Trunk** | Integrates with your existing telephony carrier through SIP. |

## Engine Types

When you create a Voice Agent you pick one of two engines:

### Simple Prompt

A single natural-language instruction block tells the agent how to behave on every call. Best when the conversation is free-form but follows consistent objectives — information collection, FAQ, qualification.

### Conversational Flow

A node-based visual designer where each node is a stage of the call (Greeting, Authentication, Card Lock, Wrap-up, etc.) with its own prompt and transition rules. Best for strictly scripted flows such as banking operations, compliance-driven collections, or multi-branch IVR replacement.

## Key Capabilities

Every Voice Agent supports:

- **Multi-language STT & TTS** — Vietnamese, English, and other regional languages.
- **Interruption handling (barge-in)** — the caller can cut the agent off naturally.
- **Backchannel cues** — short verbal acknowledgements while the caller speaks.
- **Silence follow-ups** — the agent re-engages if the caller goes quiet.
- **Tool calling** — verify phone numbers, normalize addresses, look up records, trigger workflows.
- **Live call transfer** — hand off to a human queue with context.
- **Voicemail detection** — leave a scripted message when a machine answers.
- **Knowledge base grounding** — answers stay on-topic and accurate.
- **Call analysis & summary** — structured extraction, sentiment, and disconnection reason.
- **Webhooks** — push call events and results to your CRM or data warehouse.
