title: "Live Calls Board"
description: "Watch, listen to, and take over any AI voice call in progress — for team supervisors and leads monitoring live call activity."
---

Every AI voice call your team is running is invisible by default — you find out how it went after it ends, from Call History. The Live Calls board removes that lag: it shows every call in progress right now, and lets a supervisor drop into any one of them to listen, read a rolling transcript and summary, and step in as a human if the call needs it.

## Finding the board

**Live Calls** lives as a top-level item in the left sidebar, at `/dashboard/{{team}}/live-calls`. It's gated by the same **Manage History** permission that already controls Call History access — there's no separate permission to grant. See [Roles and Permissions](/governance-access/roles-and-permissions) for who holds it on your team.

The sidebar item carries a live badge showing the current number of in-progress calls across the team, so you can see there's something to watch without opening the page.

<Note>
The board lists calls from every voice agent on the team, regardless of configuration. But you can only open a call's detail page — to listen, watch the transcript, or take over — if that agent has **Live Observability** turned on. See [Live Observability requirement](#live-observability-requirement) below.
</Note>

## The board

The board's subtitle sets the expectation: "Every AI voice call in progress for this team, updated in real time." Above the table, a stat strip shows a pulsing dot and a live count — "1 call live now" for a single call, or "`{N}` calls live now" for more — and while calls are active it appends a breakdown, e.g. "· 2 inbound · 1 outbound".

The table lists one row per in-progress call, with these columns:

| Column | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Caller** | The caller's name if known, otherwise their number. |
| **Agent** | The voice agent handling the call. |
| **Type** | The kind of call (e.g. campaign, hotline). |
| **Direction** | Inbound or outbound. |
| **Duration** | Elapsed call time, ticking live. |
| **Status** | A color-coded pill — see [Status badges](#status-badges) below. |

Click a row, or focus it and press Enter, to open that call's detail page. It opens in a **new tab**, so the board itself stays put — useful when you're keeping half an eye on several calls at once.

When nothing is in progress, the table is replaced by an empty state: a phone-off icon and "No calls in progress."

### Status badges

Each status pill pairs an icon with a label — never color alone, so the board stays readable for colorblind users.

| Status | Color | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Live** | Green, pulsing | The AI is handling the call normally. |
| **Taken Over** | Amber | A human has taken over from the AI — see [Take Over](#take-over). |
| **Stale** | Gray | The call hasn't reported activity recently; treat it as unreliable rather than trustworthy. |
| **Ended** | Gray | The call has finished but hasn't dropped off the board yet. |

## Opening a call

A call's detail page lives at `/dashboard/{{team}}/live-calls/{{room}}`. The header has a back arrow to the board, the caller's name or number with a direction badge and status badge, a subtitle showing "`{{agentName}}` · `{{duration}}`", and a **View full** link in the top right that opens the call's full Call History record in a new tab — reach for that once the call ends and you want the saved transcript and analysis.

A side panel gives you the context you'd otherwise have to look up:

- **Call Info** — Type, Direction, Agent, Hotline (if one is set), and Started ("`{{duration}}` ago").
- **Contact** — Name (or "Unknown caller") and Phone.

<Tip>
Opening a call's detail page never interrupts it. You join the room as a silent, listen-only observer of its data — the caller and agent have no idea you're there unless you turn on Listen In or take over.
</Tip>

### Transcript and summary

The center of the page is a live transcript, updating turn by turn as they're spoken — including a human agent's turns if someone has taken over. On open, you get a one-time backfill of everything said so far, capped at the last 200 lines of dialogue, so an unusually long call won't replay its full history the moment you join.

A right-rail **Summary** panel shows a live, AI-generated rolling summary of the call, refreshed roughly every 30 seconds. It first appears about 3 seconds after the call starts; before that, it reads "Summary appears once the call gets going."

## Supervisor controls

A floating control bar sits over the transcript. What it shows depends on whether you've taken over the call.

### Before taking over

| Control | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Listen In** | An ear-icon toggle that streams the live call audio to you. It flips to **Listening** (amber, pulsing) while on. |
| **Take Over** | Opens a confirmation dialog — "Take over this call?" / "You'll join as a live participant with your microphone on, replacing the assistant for the rest of the call. The caller will hear you." Confirming with **Take Over** joins you into the call. |
| **End Call** | Opens a confirmation dialog — "End this call?" / "This disconnects everyone on the call immediately. This can't be undone." Confirming with **End Call** hangs up for everyone. |

**End Call** isn't gated by any per-agent setting beyond the board's own access requirements (Manage History plus Live Observability on the agent) — anyone who can open the call can end it. Use it deliberately.

<Warning>
**Listen In** has no caller-facing disclosure or consent notice today — the caller is never told a supervisor has joined to listen. Treat this as a compliance consideration before you turn it on, especially in regulated contexts like collections, healthcare, or financial services, where call-monitoring disclosure may be a legal requirement rather than a nicety. Build your own notice into agent scripting or team policy until this ships natively.
</Warning>

### Take Over

Taking over reuses the same in-app transfer machinery documented on [Call Transfer](/voice-agents/call-transfer) — you become the equivalent of an accepted **Revve User** transfer: the AI goes silent for the rest of the call but keeps logging, and the caller now hears you live, microphone open.

Once you've taken over, the control bar switches to:

| Control | What it does |
| --- | --- |
| **Mute / Unmute** | Toggles your microphone. |
| **Hang Up** | Ends the call for everyone, same effect as End Call. |

## Live Observability requirement

The board lists calls from every agent on the team, but a call is only enterable — for Listen In, transcript viewing, or Take Over — if its agent has **Live Observability** turned on. That's a per-agent toggle under **Advanced Settings** → **Security**, separate from the Manage History permission that gates the board itself. If an agent doesn't have it on, the detail page's token-grant request is rejected (403) and you can't open the call.

See the Security section of [Voice Agent Advanced Capabilities](/voice-agents/voice-agent-advanced-capabilities#security) to turn it on for an agent — do it for anything a supervisor needs a real-time safety net on: a new campaign, a high-stakes flow, or an agent still being tuned.

## What this release doesn't show

This first release of the board and detail page shows caller, agent, duration, status, transcript, and summary — it doesn't show which conversation step the agent is on, or a live sentiment indicator.
