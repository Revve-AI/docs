The Live Calls Board is a real-time view of every AI voice call your team currently has in progress. From the board, you can open a call in detail to listen to live audio, take over as a human participant, or end the call outright. It's the tool for a supervisor watching call volume in the moment — not for building or configuring agents.

## How it works

1. Turn on **Live Observability** on each Voice Agent whose calls should be watchable (see [Turn on Live Observability](#turn-on-live-observability) below). Agents with this off never appear on the board.
2. Open **Live Calls** in the left nav. The board lists every in-progress call across the team, updated in real time.
3. Click a row to open the call detail page in a new tab.
4. From the detail page, **Listen In** to hear live audio, **Take Over** to join as a human participant, or **End Call** to disconnect everyone.

### Turn on Live Observability

Live Observability lives on a Voice Agent's **Security Settings** tab, off by default. In the app it's described as: "Allow team members to watch, listen to, and take over this agent's calls from the Live Calls board."

If it's off for an agent, that agent's calls are skipped from the board entirely — not just hidden, but never added. Since this is the entry point for the whole feature, turn it on per agent before expecting to see anything: it isn't a team-wide switch.

<Note>
Live Observability only gates **Listen In** (hearing live audio). **Take Over** and **End Call** are not gated by this setting — see [Listen In vs. Take Over vs. End Call](#listen-in-vs-take-over-vs-end-call) below.
</Note>

## The board

**Live Calls** sits in the left nav between Tickets and Campaigns, marked with a radio icon and a live badge count of calls currently in progress. It's visible to any team member with the `MANAGE_HISTORY` permission.

The board header reads "Every AI voice call in progress for this team, updated in real time," followed by a summary line — "N calls live now" (singular "1 call live now"), then a breakdown like "3 inbound · 2 outbound." A pulsing green dot appears next to the summary while calls are live. When there's nothing in progress, the board shows "No calls in progress."

The table columns, in order:

| Column | What it shows |
|--------|----------------|
| **Caller** | The caller's identity (name or number). |
| **Agent** | Which Voice Agent is handling the call. |
| **Type** | The call type. |
| **Direction** | Inbound or outbound. |
| **Duration** | Elapsed call time, live-updating. |
| **Status** | One of the four states below. |

Status values:

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Live** (green, pulsing) | The AI is handling the call; no human is involved. |
| **Taken Over** (amber) | A human is now on the line. |
| **Stale** (gray) | No recent update from the call — usually a webhook delay, not necessarily a dead call. |
| **Ended** (gray) | The call has finished. |

Clicking a row opens the call's detail page in a new browser tab.

<Note>
A row is marked **Stale** 60 seconds after its last webhook event, and dropped from the board entirely 120 seconds after its last event if no `room_finished` webhook ever arrives. A server-side reconciliation job also cleans these up as a backstop, so a row shouldn't get permanently stuck — if a call vanishes from the board, this staleness rule is almost always why, not a bug.
</Note>

## The call detail page

Opening a call joins you to its LiveKit room as a hidden, subscribe-only observer: you get the live transcript and can act on the call, but the caller and agent can't hear or see you unless you explicitly take over.

A floating control bar gives you three controls while observing:

| Control | What it does |
|---------|---------------|
| **Listen In** | A toggle. Once active, it's shown pressed and amber-tinted, and its label changes to "Listening" — you hear the live call audio. Requires Live Observability on for that agent. |
| **Take Over** | Opens a confirm dialog: "Take over this call?" / "You'll join as a live participant with your microphone on, replacing the assistant for the rest of the call. The caller will hear you." Confirm with **Take Over**. |
| **End Call** | Opens a confirm dialog: "End this call?" / "This disconnects everyone on the call immediately. This can't be undone." Confirm with **End Call**. |

Once you take over, the control bar switches to standard live-participant controls: **Mute**/**Unmute** and **Hang Up**.

The transcript panel shows the live conversation as it happens — what the AI said and heard, and once you take over, your turns are labeled **Human Agent** to distinguish them from **Assistant** turns. A right-hand panel shows an AI-generated summary of the call so far, refreshed roughly every 30 seconds, so you can get oriented on a call you're jumping into mid-conversation without reading the full transcript.

### Listen In vs. Take Over vs. End Call

**Listen In** is gated on the agent's **Live Observability** setting — if it's off, you can't listen. **Take Over** and **End Call** are not gated on that setting at all. Any team member with the `MANAGE_HISTORY` permission (i.e. anyone who can see the board) can take over or end any live call for the team, regardless of whether that specific agent has Live Observability enabled.

This is deliberate: taking over or ending a call is a team-supervision right, separate from whether audio monitoring is allowed for that agent. In practice this means a team member can end a call for an agent they aren't permitted to listen in on.

## Live Calls vs. Human Takeover vs. Call Transfer

Revve has three mechanisms with related names — worth telling apart:

- **Live Calls Board** (this page) — a supervisor observing or intervening in a voice call from the outside, via a LiveKit room join. Governed by `MANAGE_HISTORY` and per-agent Live Observability.
- **[Human Takeover](/inbox/human-takeover)** — the chat/text equivalent: an operator claims a conversation thread in the Inbox. Different transport (chat thread, not a call room) and a different permission model.
- **[Call Transfer](/voice-agents/call-transfer)** — the AI agent proactively hands the call to a human *during the call flow itself*, because the caller needs one. The caller is being routed, not observed. Live Calls Board is the opposite direction: a supervisor reaching in from outside, not the agent reaching out.

## When to Take Over vs. End Call

Both are available to any supervisor watching the board, but they're for different situations:

- **Take Over** when the caller needs a human to actually resolve something and you have the bandwidth to stay on the line for the rest of the call. You're replacing the assistant, not just pausing it — plan to see the call through.
- **End Call** when the call has gone wrong — a stuck loop, a distressed or abusive caller, a compliance problem — and there's no path to a good outcome, or no one available to take over. It's a hard stop with no undo, so use it when continuing the call (AI or human) is worse than ending it.
